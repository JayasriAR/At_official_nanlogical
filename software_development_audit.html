Redwerk offers the software development lifecycle (SDLC) audits and efficiency evaluation services so that you can be sure that every cent you invest benefits your project, as well as, no time is wasted due to inefficient or unnecessary procedures. When it comes to the implementation of big projects and grand ideas – it is always easy to sketch a clear objective, and fairly difficult to select the appropriate techniques, tools, documents, and procedures through which an idea becomes a supported product. However, most problems come about not because of a lack of talent or creativity, but because the available methods and processes are not used in an efficient way or in a way which is unrelated to the final product.

Evaluating development processes and project management flow, Redwek’s savvy advisors accompany one along the way to the product that meets the requirements and fulfills the needs. Being in close collaboration with the developers and their managers we help streamline the pipeline phases to make sure everything is 100% optimized.

The Main Steps Of The SD Audit Process

Redwerk’s major objective is to assist development teams in improving software quality and productivity through unbiased assessment and evaluation of their software development process, thus saving time and money for the customers.



Redwerk’s SD Audit Process Includes

Initiating the audit

Defining the mission, identifying the stakeholders, planning the audit to highlight the objectives in terms of success factors.
Analyzing the organization

General information, the role of SD in the organization, technologies, platforms, tools, and paradigms – all these contribute to the satisfactory outcome.
Evaluating SD processes

Plunging into the documentation, throwing surveys, conducting the interviews with team members, identifying client-team-management perspective on a 360 degrees assessment to get a reliable description of the reality.
Evaluating organizational structure

Employees’ satisfaction, relevant organizational structure are the key factors for motivation, productivity, hence the expected product outcome.
Evaluating tools and technologies

The chosen tools and technologies for project management, bug tracking, customer service or CRM, automated testing, etc. must be appropriate and efficient.efining the mission, identifying the stakeholders, planning the audit to highlight the objectives in terms of success factors.
Reporting audit results

At this stage, we provide management and stakeholders with an audit report, present the recommendations, and implementation plan helping them in making decisions
Following up

To reap the full benefits of the SD audit we design a plan of engagements determining subsequent reviews to make sure all the recommendations are being implemented.
